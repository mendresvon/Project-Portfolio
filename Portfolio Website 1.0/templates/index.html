My Way of Thanking CS50

August 22, 2022

Talisay City, Cebu

Philippines 6045

I would love to take this opportunity to thank you and all of the CS50 team for designing and building this amazing course. Thank you Sir David Malan for having done an amazing job in teaching computer science. I know that many people have already said this to you but you, Sir David, are one of the best teachers I could have ever hoped for. You make learning fun, engaging, exciting, and I couldn't help but wish that I was actually there. To be honest, I found Computer Science daunting at first. I thought that it would be too hard for me. However, through your lectures coupled with your radiating enthusiasm topped with your oozing passion to teach, I was moved. I came to love Computer Science due to the fact that I got to experience this amazing opportunity to learn from an equally amazing teacher like you.

I am a learner from the Philippines and I'm going to college soon! Yay! I recently graduated highschool and I found a scholarship to study abroad in Taiwan. I plan on taking a double major in Mechanical Engineering and Computer Science. I know.... it sounds like suicide xD. However, I am actually serious with this. I mean, I wish that I could go to Harvard and actually meet you but that boat has already sailed. That said, the scholarship that I have ain't too bad. I basically get to study for cheap. AND earn two majors! I want to someday work at Tesla or any of the Silicon Valley Companies. If given the chance, I would also love to be a staff at Harvard University's CS50 Course hehe.

Once again, I would love to thank you. You have changed my life more than you can ever imagine. Through CS50, I now have a path that I would actually love to take. I would make it my life mission to meet you one day. One day. Some day. Thank you so much from the deepest depths of my heart. I know you probably won't be able to read this due to the number of projects you receive, but I'll remain hopeful. In case you read this, I'll leave my email and phone number at the bottom of the page :). Once again, thank you Harvard, thank you CS50, and most especially, THANK YOU SIR DAVID.

Love from the Philippines,

Von Breznev A. Mendres
